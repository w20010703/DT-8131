Project Question

How can we help socially anxious dating app users take their passive online engagement?

Current knowledge

Dating apps are dominated by digital-only interactions (text, swipe-based).
Socially anxious users often create accounts but avoid initiating or replying, leading to disengagement.
Existing solutions (icebreaker prompts, algorithmic matching, “boosts”) mainly stay within digital space and don’t address the leap into physical/social presence.
Hybrid/physical dating events exist (speed dating, group meetups), but they require high initial effort and rarely integrate seamlessly with apps.

Areas to develop

Explore hybrid digital-physical triggers (wearables, AR cues, location-based micro-interactions).
Understand behavioral psychology of anxiety reduction in first-contact moments.
Look into social signaling mechanisms (e.g., subtle nonverbal cues, physical artifacts, or “permission slips” from the app).

Intent of Purpose to ‘Radical Prototype’

Inspired by Radical Software’s push for media as agency rather than passive consumption, the prototype could break the digital-only model of dating apps.

Speculative radical prototype idea:

A “phygital token” system: when two matched users are in physical proximity, their phones (or wearable objects) subtly activate a shared artifact (e.g., a flower pin lights up, a table coaster glows, or a screen shows a blooming visual).
This forces an ambient physical acknowledgement without direct initiation, lowering the barrier for socially anxious people.
The prototype radically reframes dating apps from “private chatrooms” to public/shared micro-interactions that can spill into real life.
