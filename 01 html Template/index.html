IDEA1: Dating App & Social Anxiety
Project Question

How can we help socially anxious dating app users take their passive online engagement?

State of Knowledge of the Field
      Current Knowledge
  • Dating apps are dominated by digital-only interactions (text, swipe-based).
  • Socially anxious users often create accounts but avoid initiating or replying, leading to disengagement.
  • Existing solutions (icebreaker prompts, algorithmic matching, “boosts”) mainly stay within digital space and don’t address the leap into physical/social presence.
      Areas to Develop
  • Explore hybrid digital-physical triggers
  • Understand the behavioral psychology of anxiety reduction in first-contact moments.
Intent of Purpose to ‘Radical Prototype’

A prototype could break the digital-only model of dating apps.

Speculative radical prototype idea

A Dating App version of Tamagotchi, where each user has a virtual pet that can only be retrieved after meeting their match offline. The pet “visits” another user’s home during pairing, forcing the first encounter to happen in person. The shared pet becomes a playful icebreaker, turning the first physical meeting into a natural social interaction.




IDEA2: Multi-Robot Collaboration
Project Question

How might we enable multiple robots to divide tasks and collaborate efficiently in order to accomplish a complex overall goal?

State of Knowledge of the Field
      Current Knowledge
  • Multi-robot systems exist in logistics (warehouse robots, swarm drones).
  • Current coordination methods: centralized task allocation (a master system delegates tasks) vs decentralized swarm intelligence.
  • Household robots (Roomba, robotic vacuums) typically act independently, not collaboratively.
  • Research explores communication protocols, spatial awareness, and role assignment.
      Areas to Develop
  • Cross-over between modular robotics and swarm behavior.
  • New forms of human-robot orchestration
  • Adaptive, task-specific division of labor where robots learn to negotiate roles dynamically.
Intent of Purpose to ‘Radical Prototype’

The prototype that challenges centralized robot control.

Speculative radical prototype idea

A decentralized “robot orchestra” where each robot functions like an instrument in a larger score. For example, in a cleaning swarm, one robot sprays, another brushes, and another vacuums. Each module can operate independently or in combination.
