IDEA: Dating App & Social Anxiety
Project Question

How can we help socially anxious dating app users take their passive online engagement?

State of Knowledge of the Field
      Current Knowledge
  • Dating apps are dominated by digital-only interactions (text, swipe-based).
  • Socially anxious users often create accounts but avoid initiating or replying, leading to disengagement.
  • Existing solutions (icebreaker prompts, algorithmic matching, “boosts”) mainly stay within digital space and don’t address the leap into physical/social presence.
      Areas to Develop
  • Explore hybrid digital-physical triggers
  • Understand the behavioral psychology of anxiety reduction in first-contact moments.
Map of Community of Practice
  • Hinge, Bumble, Tinder – commercial dating platforms experimenting with prompts and safety features.
  • Academic researchers in HCI studying digital intimacy and social anxiety.
  • Therapists and support groups focusing on exposure therapy for social anxiety.
Research Methods
  • User interviews with socially anxious dating app users.
  • Behavioral observation of online/offline engagement transitions.
  • Prototyping and user testing hybrid interaction models.
Technology Ecosystem Diagram
  • Software: Mobile app (iOS/Android), Python backend server.
  • Hardware: Smartphones, optional IoT devices for physical triggers (e.g., RFID tags, wearables).
Intent of Purpose to ‘Radical Prototype’

A prototype could break the digital-only model of dating apps.

Speculative radical prototype idea

A Dating App version of Tamagotchi, where each user has a virtual pet that can only be retrieved after meeting their match offline. The pet “visits” another user’s home during pairing, forcing the first encounter to happen in person. The shared pet becomes a playful icebreaker, turning the first physical meeting into a natural social interaction.
